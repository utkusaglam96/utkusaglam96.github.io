Research

My current research is focused on collective quantum interaction experiments on Rb atoms. We cool down the atoms in a Magneto Optical Trap (MOT) with doppler and sisyphus cooling and perform collective emission and absorption experiments. I started by assembling a External cavity diode lasers (ECDL) and saturaion absorption spectroscopy setups for laser locking. Later on, I worked a lot on designing and assembling an analog lockbox for the lasers. Later on, we set up the optics for the MOT and gradually worked on the MOT to make it cooler. In the October of 2024, we managed to trap our atoms on a far off resonant optical trap. In the Spring of 2025, we got our first optical lattices. We are currently working on making more tightly confined optical lattices to observe the colelctive quantum interactions of the atoms.

I started working the lab with a project on measuring the whispering gallery modes of a CaF2 crystal with a 1550 nm laser. Before moving in to the lab, I worked on some theoretical work including numerically designing a neutral atom-based phase gate, various applications of the non-transverse modes within optical fibers and Chiral Quantum Walks.
